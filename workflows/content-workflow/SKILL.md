# Content Workflow

Complete workflow for creating high-quality marketing copy and content for SaaS applications.

## Use Case

Use this workflow when:
- Creating marketing copy for a SaaS landing page
- Writing product descriptions and feature pages
- Developing pricing pages and CTA copy
- Creating about pages and company descriptions
- Need persuasive, conversion-focused content
- Writing blog posts and thought leadership content

## Workflow Overview

This workflow guides you through creating marketing content in 6 steps:

1. **Research & Strategy** - Understand audience, product, and goals
2. **Structure & Outline** - Create content structure and messaging hierarchy
3. **Draft Content** - Write compelling copy with proven frameworks
4. **Optimize & Refine** - Enhance for SEO, conversion, and readability
5. **Review & Test** - Validate copy against goals and audience
6. **Finalize & Deploy** - Prepare content for production

## Prerequisites Skills

This workflow calls these skills conditionally based on your needs:
- `content/copywriting` - Marketing copywriting principles (ALWAYS)

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Step 1: Research & Strategy

#### 1.1 Define Target Audience

**Questions to answer**:
- Who is the ideal customer persona?
- What are their pain points, challenges, and desires?
- What's their current solution?
- What motivates them to take action?
- What objections do they have?

**Create audience profile**:

```markdown
## Persona: [Name]

### Demographics
- Age: [range]
- Role: [job title/industry]
- Experience level: [beginner/intermediate/expert]

### Psychographics
- Goals: [what they want to achieve]
- Pain points: [problems they face]
- Motivations: [what drives them]
- Objections: [what stops them from buying]

### Context
- Current solution: [what they use now]
- Budget: [price range]
- Decision factors: [what matters most]
```

#### 1.2 Understand Product/Service

**Key questions**:
- What problem does your product solve?
- How does it solve it differently?
- What are the top 3 benefits (not features)?
- What social proof exists (testimonials, case studies)?
- What makes your solution unique?

**Create value proposition**:

```markdown
## Value Proposition

**For [target audience]**

Who [pain point/challenge],

**[Product name]** is a [category of product]

That [key differentiation].

Unlike [alternative solution],

We provide [unique advantage/benefit].

### Key Benefits
1. [Benefit 1] - [How it helps]
2. [Benefit 2] - [How it helps]
3. [Benefit 3] - [How it helps]

### Social Proof
- [Testimonial 1]
- [Case study 1]
- [Metric/Stat]
```

#### 1.3 Define Content Goals

**Set SMART goals**:

| Goal Type | Specific | Measurable | Achievable | Relevant | Time-bound |
|-----------|----------|-------------|------------|----------|------------|
| **Conversion** | Increase sign-ups | +25% | In 3 months | Focus on landing page | End of Q2 |
| **Engagement** | Reduce bounce rate | -15% | Improve copy | Better user experience | In 2 months |
| **SEO** | Rank for keywords | Top 3 | Quality content | Increase organic traffic | In 6 months |

### Step 2: Structure & Outline

#### 2.1 Content Hierarchy

**Create messaging pyramid**:

```
                    [Primary Message]
                           |
        ---------------------------------------
        |             |                  |
   [Benefit 1]  [Benefit 2]       [Benefit 3]
        |             |                  |
   [Feature A]  [Feature B]       [Feature C]
        |             |                  |
   [Detail 1]  [Detail 2]       [Detail 3]
```

#### 2.2 Page Structure

**Standard landing page structure**:

```markdown
1. Hero Section
   - Headline: [Primary benefit in 8-12 words]
   - Subheadline: [Elaborate on primary benefit]
   - CTA: [Action-oriented button text]

2. Problem/Agitation
   - Describe the problem
   - Amplify the pain
   - Show consequences

3. Solution
   - Introduce your product
   - Explain how it works
   - Show key differentiators

4. Benefits
   - Benefit 1 with details
   - Benefit 2 with details
   - Benefit 3 with details

5. Social Proof
   - Testimonials
   - Case studies
   - Logos/Trust signals

6. Features
   - Key features in detail
   - How each feature benefits user

7. Pricing
   - Pricing tiers
   - What's included
   - Call to action

8. FAQ
   - Common questions
   - Address objections

9. Final CTA
   - Restate main benefit
   - Strong call to action
```

#### 2.3 Copy Frameworks

**Use proven frameworks**:

##### AIDA Framework
- **Attention**: Hook reader with compelling headline
- **Interest**: Build interest with relevant content
- **Desire**: Create desire through benefits
- **Action**: Call to action

##### PAS Framework
- **Problem**: Describe the problem
- **Agitation**: Agitate the pain
- **Solution**: Present your solution

##### 4P Framework
- **Promise**: Make a promise
- **Picture**: Paint a picture of results
- **Proof**: Provide proof of effectiveness
- **Push**: Push to action

### Step 3: Draft Content

#### 3.1 Write Compelling Headlines

**Headline formulas**:

```markdown
## Benefit-Focused
- How to [achieve desired result]
- The secret to [desired outcome]
- Get [benefit] in [timeframe]

## Number-Based
- 7 ways to [benefit]
- 5 [things] that will [result]
- 3 reasons why [statement]

## Question-Based
- Want to [benefit]?
- Struggling with [problem]?
- Tired of [pain point]?

## How-To
- How to [benefit] in [time]
- How I [achieved result]
- How [industry leaders] [achieve result]
```

#### 3.2 Write Persuasive Body Copy

**Writing principles**:

```markdown
## Be Specific
- Instead of "fast results" → "3x faster deployment"
- Instead of "many users" → "10,000+ teams"
- Instead of "easy to use" → "Setup in 5 minutes"

## Focus on Benefits, Not Features
- Feature: "Dark mode" → Benefit: "Work comfortably any time of day"
- Feature: "API access" → Benefit: "Integrate with your existing tools"
- Feature: "Unlimited projects" → Benefit: "Scale without limits"

## Use Power Words
- Transform, Revolutionize, Dominate, Master
- Discover, Unlock, Reveal, Expose
- Boost, Accelerate, Amplify, Maximize
- Eliminate, Eradicate, Crush, Obliterate

## Use Social Proof
- "Join 10,000+ teams already using [product]"
- "4.9/5 stars from 500+ reviews"
- "Trusted by industry leaders like [company names]"

## Address Objections
- "No credit card required"
- "Cancel anytime"
- "24/7 support included"
- "30-day money-back guarantee"
```

#### 3.3 Write Strong CTAs

**CTA best practices**:

```markdown
## Action-Oriented
- Get Started Free
- Start Your Free Trial

- Unlock Full Access

## Benefit-Focused

- Only 5 Spots Left
```

### Step 4: Optimize & Refine

#### 4.1 SEO Optimization

**Keyword integration**:

```markdown
## Primary Keywords
- [Main keyword]
- [Secondary keyword]
- [Long-tail keyword]

## Keyword Placement
- Headline: Include primary keyword
- Subheadlines: Include secondary keywords
- First paragraph: Include primary keyword naturally
- Throughout: Mention keywords 2-3% of content
- Meta description: Include primary keyword
- URL slug: Use primary keyword

## On-Page SEO
- Use H1, H2, H3 tags hierarchically
- Include internal links to relevant pages
- Optimize images with alt text
- Use descriptive URLs
- Add meta description (155-160 characters)
```

#### 4.2 Readability Optimization

**Improving readability**:

```markdown
## Structure
- Use short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Use bullet points and numbered lists
- Break up text with subheadlines
- Use white space effectively

## Sentence Length
- Keep sentences under 20 words
- Avoid complex sentence structures
- Use active voice
- Remove unnecessary words

## Vocabulary
- Use simple, familiar words
- Avoid jargon and technical terms
- Explain technical concepts if needed
- Use analogies and metaphors

## Formatting
- Use bold for emphasis
- Use italics for book titles, foreign words
- Use blockquotes for testimonials
- Use code blocks for technical content
```

#### 4.3 Conversion Optimization

**Improving conversions**:

```markdown
## Remove Friction
- Simplify forms (fewer fields)
- Clear navigation
- Fast loading times
- Mobile-responsive design

## Build Trust
- Add testimonials and reviews
- Show trust badges and certifications
- Display security indicators
- Show real user photos

## Reduce Anxiety
- Money-back guarantee
- Free trial
- No credit card required
- Clear refund policy

## Create Urgency
- Limited-time offers
- Countdown timers
- Scarcity (limited spots)
- Exclusive bonuses
```

**Testing methods**:

```markdown

- Test headline variations
- Test CTA button text
- Test page layouts
- Test images vs. text

## Heatmaps
- See where users click
- Identify scroll depth
- Find friction points

## User Feedback
- Survey users
- Collect testimonials
- Ask for suggestions

## Analytics
- Track conversion rates
- Monitor bounce rates
- Analyze time on page
- Measure engagement
```

### Step 6: Finalize & Deploy

#### 6.1 Final Polish

```markdown
## Proofreading
- Check for typos and errors
- Verify links work
- Ensure formatting is correct
- Check mobile responsiveness

## Meta Data
- Update meta title (50-60 characters)
- Update meta description (155-160 characters)
- Add social media tags (Open Graph, Twitter Cards)
- Update alt text for images

## Technical Setup
- Set up tracking (Google Analytics)
- Add conversion tracking
- Configure heatmaps
- Set up A/B testing
```

#### 6.2 Launch Checklist

## Common Pitfalls

### 1. Writing Features Instead of Benefits
```markdown
# ❌ BAD - Feature-focused
"Our product includes dark mode, API access, and unlimited projects."

# ✅ GOOD - Benefit-focused
"Work comfortably any time of day, integrate with your existing tools, and scale without limits."
```

### 2. Not Being Specific
```markdown
# ❌ BAD - Vague claims
"Our product is fast and easy to use."

# ✅ GOOD - Specific claims
"Our product deploys 3x faster and can be set up in just 5 minutes."
```

### 3. Not Addressing Objections
```markdown
# ❌ BAD - No objection handling
"Start your free trial today."

# ✅ GOOD - Addressing objections
"Start your free trial today. No credit card required, cancel anytime, 30-day money-back guarantee."
```

### 4. Not Including Social Proof
```markdown
# ❌ BAD - No social proof
"Our product is the best."

# ✅ GOOD - With social proof
"Trusted by 10,000+ teams with a 4.9/5 star rating from 500+ reviews."
```

### 5. Not Using Strong CTAs
```markdown
# ❌ BAD - Weak CTA
"Learn more"

# ✅ GOOD - Strong CTA
"Start your free trial and boost your productivity today."
```
